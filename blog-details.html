Becoming Remote-First As An
International Company

How COVID-19 has changed the international work environment

The term ‘new normal’ has become something of a pet hate for many people in recent months, we use it to deflect the uncomfortable and hyper paced change that has been caused by the Covid-19 pandemic. The virus swept across the globe, engulfing everything in its path including how and where we work. 2020 may have been exceptional in regards to the pace of change and how we live and interact with each other. It is by no means the first nor the last global challenge we will face, there is one undeniable truth that follows every disaster, those that adapt the fastest to change are the ones that come through as winners. Just consider, before the 2008 financial crash, Netflix, AirBnB and YouTube were still just whispers and by no means household names. ‘Of the 100 companies listed on our annual America’s Most Promising List, around a third were forged in the doom and gloom years of the global financial crisis’ says Hollie Slade of Forbes. Past Global events have produced winners and losers, this global pandemic will be no different. The most noticeable change will be how and more importantly where we choose to live and work, combining the two is now our ‘new normal’.

Why it makes sense to become remote-first

The list of reasons to become remote-first is growing as the practice produces positive results. A few benefits stand out loud and clear. These include business continuity, top talent recruiting and retention, financial savings, employee wellness, long-term flexibility and adaptability. There are certainly some businesses that require some degree of face-to-face interaction, albeit partially covered by a facemask. Modern technologies and business culture have made remote-first a preferable way of working for many leading companies be it large or small. The option to work remote could be viewed as leveling the playing field for smaller companies. No longer does attracting the best talent in an industry rely on the financial might of the larger companies. SMEs can now acquire the best talent by offering a remote work option without the substantial costs of setting up legally compliant entities in each country. SMEs’ CEOs and HR managers are now looking at this situation and thinking ‘Game on’.

How the decision of "becoming remote-first" creates unforeseen challenges

Setting up business in another country can be daunting. Developing an in-depth understanding of a country’s laws, politics, culture and compliance can take a long time and significant investment. The risks associated with this method of global expansion can be enough to keep even the hardiest of CEOs and HR managers awake at night.

Summary

Our expectations of being permitted to work away from the office have changed and some of this culture change will stick, particularly if companies see their rivals winning clients and closing deals as a result of their agile, remote-first policy. Attracting the world's top talent is no longer reserved for the big companies, with PeopleFlow there is now a level playing field and the next generation of household names will soon emerge from this global crisis and they will be powered by remote-first teams.
